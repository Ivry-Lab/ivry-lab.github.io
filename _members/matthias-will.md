I am a Postdoctoral Researcher interested in how we learn and adjust movement and the underlying mental operations that shape that learning. My work explores the interplay between automatic and strategic processes in motor learning, and how perception and cognition influence the way we refine movement. I am also interested in the role of sensory and cerebellar signals in supporting this learning. To investigate these questions, I use behavioral paradigms combined with neural imaging and stimulation techniques, working with both healthy individuals and clinical populations.
